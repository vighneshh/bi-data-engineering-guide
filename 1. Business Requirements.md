# 📋 BI Dashboard Development - Step 1: Business Requirements

The **Business Requirements** phase is the foundation of every BI dashboard. Before writing a single line of code or designing visuals, answer the following questions to ensure the dashboard solves the right business problem.

---

## 🎯 1. Business Objective

* What business problem am I trying to solve?
* Why is this dashboard needed?
* What decisions should this dashboard help users make?
* What is the expected business outcome?

---

## 👥 2. Stakeholders

* Who requested the dashboard?
* Who are the primary users?
* What are their roles? (Executive, Manager, Analyst, Operations, etc.)
* What are their expectations?

---

## ❓ 3. Business Questions

* What questions should this dashboard answer?
* What insights are users expecting?
* What actions should users take based on these insights?
* Which business processes should be monitored?

---

## 📊 4. KPIs & Metrics

* Which KPIs are required?
* How is each KPI calculated?
* Are there any business rules or formulas?
* Which KPIs are mandatory and which are optional?
* Are there target values or benchmarks?

---

## 🗂️ 5. Dimensions

* Which dimensions are needed for analysis?

  * Date
  * Year
  * Month
  * Country
  * Region
  * Customer
  * Product
  * ERP
  * Department
* Are there any hierarchies?

  * Region → Country
  * Year → Quarter → Month

---

## 🎛️ 6. Filters & Slicers

* Which filters should be available?
* Should filters be global or page-specific?
* What should be the default selections?
* Should users be able to select multiple values?

---

## 🔍 7. Drill-down & Navigation

* Is drill-down required?
* What should the drill path be?
* Should users navigate between summary and detailed views?
* Are drill-through pages required?

---

## 📈 8. Visualizations

* Which KPIs should be displayed as KPI cards?
* Which charts best represent the data?

  * Bar Chart
  * Line Chart
  * Pie/Donut Chart
  * Table
  * Pivot Table
  * Map
  * Scatter Plot
* Are there any preferred dashboard layouts or wireframes?

---

## 🗄️ 9. Data Sources

* Where does the data come from?
* How many source systems are involved?
* Who owns each data source?
* Is data stored in SQL, Excel, APIs, ERP systems, or Cloud platforms?

---

## ✅ 10. Data Quality

* Is the source data complete?
* Are there duplicate records?
* Are there missing values?
* Are there any known data quality issues?
* How should invalid or missing data be handled?

---

## 🔄 11. Refresh Requirements

* How often should the dashboard refresh?
* Is real-time reporting required?
* What is the acceptable data latency?
* Should refreshes be scheduled or manual?

---

## 🔐 12. Security & Access

* Who should have access to the dashboard?
* Is Row-Level Security (RLS) required?
* Should users only see data for their region, country, or department?
* Are there different permission levels?

---

## ⚡ 13. Performance

* What is the expected dashboard load time?
* How much data will be loaded?
* Are there any performance constraints?
* Is dashboard optimization required?

---

## 📤 14. Reporting Requirements

* Should users export data to Excel or PDF?
* Is printing required?
* Is mobile compatibility needed?
* Should reports support subscriptions or email delivery?

---

## 🚨 15. Alerts & Notifications

* Should alerts be sent when KPIs exceed thresholds?
* Are conditional formatting rules required?
* Should notifications be automated?

---

## ✔️ 16. Acceptance Criteria

* How will stakeholders verify the dashboard?
* What conditions must be met before sign-off?
* Who will perform User Acceptance Testing (UAT)?

---

## 🚀 17. Future Enhancements

* Are additional KPIs planned?
* Will new data sources be added?
* Should the dashboard be scalable?
* Are future dashboard versions expected?

---

# ✅ Business Requirements Checklist

Use this checklist before starting dashboard development.

* [ ] Business objective is clearly defined.
* [ ] Stakeholders are identified.
* [ ] Business questions are documented.
* [ ] KPIs and calculations are finalized.
* [ ] Dimensions and hierarchies are identified.
* [ ] Filters and slicers are defined.
* [ ] Drill-down requirements are confirmed.
* [ ] Dashboard visualizations are planned.
* [ ] Data sources are identified.
* [ ] Data quality has been assessed.
* [ ] Refresh frequency is agreed upon.
* [ ] Security requirements are documented.
* [ ] Performance expectations are defined.
* [ ] Reporting and export requirements are finalized.
* [ ] Alert requirements are confirmed.
* [ ] Acceptance criteria are documented.
* [ ] Future enhancements have been noted.

---

# 💡 Pro Tip

> **If you cannot answer these questions, don't start building the dashboard yet.**
>
> Spending time on business requirements prevents rework, ensures stakeholder alignment, and results in dashboards that drive better business decisions.
